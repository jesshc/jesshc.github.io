The Radiological Accident in Goiânia

The Accident Spread & Decontamination Outlook

The Accident

Have you ever wondered what will happen if nuclear waste ever came in contact with the public? In September 1987 one of the most serious radiation accidents in history took place in Goiania, Brazil.

What happened?

On an afternoon of September 1987, two thieves stole a radiation therapy device for cancer treatment from an abandoned clinic in Goiania. Without knowing that the substance was radioactive, they removed the capsule containing the radioactive cesium-137 from its lead casing that was inside the device. Cesium-137 looked like mysterious fluorescent blue powder and the thieves didn’t know it was radioactive, so they began to distribute the glowing powder to friends and relatives. Some of them painted the powder on their shirts and used it as makeup. Then the thieves sold the capsule to a scrap dealer. The scrap dealer distributed it to his family members too. His 6-year-old niece rubbed the powder on her body like glitter and even ate a sandwich with the powder on her hands.

On the following days, these people began to experience diarrhea, vomiting, blistered hands and skin, and faintness. The scrap dealer eventullly went to the Goiania public clinic where a medical worker diagnosed radiation illness and alerted the local government.
